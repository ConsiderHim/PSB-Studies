Lesson 1: In the Beginning

True or False

  • 1. The Bible says that God was created along with the rest of creation. — False
  • 2. Genesis explains the beginnings of our universe, humanity, and the great history of our common heritage. — True
  • 3. The Bible says God created the world over millions of years. — False
  • 4. Man was created in the image of animals. — False
  • 5. You are special, unique, and crafted by the Almighty God. — True
  • 6. Woman was created to help man. — True
  • 7. God originally created man to have several wives. — False
  • 8. There is little to no responsibility in a new marriage. — False

Search the Scriptures

  • 1. In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth. (Gen 1:1)
  • 2. God saw all that He had made, and behold, it was very good. (Gen 1:31)
  • 3. I will give thanks to You, for I am fearfully and wonderfully made. (Psa 139:14)
  • 4. Therefore a man shall leave his father and mother and be joined to his wife, and they shall become one flesh. (Gen 2:24)

Deeper Connections (Preferred Answers)

  • Q: What was God’s gift of “choice,” and what were the possible outcomes of the choice?

    A: God gave Adam free will through the tree of the knowledge of good and evil. The outcomes were: obeying God leads to harmony and life; obeying Self leads to death (physical and spiritual separation).

  • Q: Explain how a husband and wife become “one flesh” and why their relationship is unique.

    A: A husband and wife share a unique physical, emotional, and spiritual relationship that they are to have only together. Marriage creates a new family unit joined by God.

  • Q: What are some possible consequences of not believing that humans are created in God’s image?

    A: It strips humanity of its holy place in God's creation, making humans seem no better than brute animals, which can lead to a loss of inherent value, purpose, and self-worth.

Lesson 2: The Patriarchs

True or False

  • 1. Noah had faith in God by obeying God’s commands. — True
  • 2. There were thousands of people who survived the flood all over the world. — False
  • 3. The people of Babel wanted to build a tower to give God glory. — False
  • 4. The different tribes and languages began as a result of the people’s prideful sin at the tower of Babel. — True
  • 5. Abraham refused to sacrifice his son on the altar. — False
  • 6. God did not ask Abraham to do anything that He was not willing to do Himself. — True
  • 7. God’s promise of a blessing for the world was fulfilled in Jesus. — True

Search the Scriptures

  • 1. If you do well, will you not be accepted? And if you do not do well, sin lies at the door. And its desire is for you, but you should rule over it. (Gen 4:7)
  • 2. Then the Lord saw that the wickedness of man was great in the earth, and that every intent of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually. (Gen 6:5)
  • 3. “Do not lay your hand on the lad, […]; for now I know that you fear God since you have not withheld your son, your only son, from Me.” (Gen 22:12)
  • 4. […] and through your offspring all nations on earth will be blessed, because you have obeyed me. (Gen 22:18)

Deeper Connections (Preferred Answers)

  • Q: How did Noah demonstrate his faith in God?

    A: By listening to and obeying God’s commands to build the ark, even when the instructions seemed strange.

  • Q: Why did God decide to flood the whole world?

    A: Because the wickedness of man was great and every intent of their thoughts was only evil continually.

  • Q: How did God’s promise to Abraham involve Jesus?

    A: Jesus came as a descendant of Abraham, fulfilling the promise that all nations would be blessed through his offspring.

  • Q: Why is it important that we know that men like Abraham struggled with sin?

    A: It shows they were human and serves as a warning that all people need redemption.

Lesson 3: Israel Enslaved and Redeemed

True or False

  • 1. Moses never struggled with sin. — False
  • 2. The Passover was a special meal and a foreshadowing example of Jesus. — True
  • 3. The Law of Moses was meant to teach Israel how to love and honor God, as well as how to love and honor their fellow man. — True
  • 4. The sacrificial system in the Law was to teach Israel that in order for sins to be forgiven, an innocent sacrifice had to die. — True
  • 5. Israel was meant to be a holy nation and a beacon of hope to the world that would bring mankind to God. — True
  • 6. God has always desired a relationship based on choice and motivated by love. — True
  • 7. Jesus did not fulfill any prophecies in the Bible. — False

Search the Scriptures

  • 1. Then the children of Israel groaned because of the bondage, and they cried out; and their cry came up to God because of the bondage. (Exo 2:23)
  • 2. You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, and with all your strength. (Deu 6:5)
  • 3. In those days there was no king in Israel: every man did that which was right in his own eyes. (Jud 21:25)
  • 4. The Lord has sought for Himself a man after His own heart, and the Lord has commanded him to be commander over His people [...] (1 Sam 13:14)
  • 5. But He was wounded for our transgressions, He was bruised for our iniquities; the chastisement for our peace was upon Him, and by His stripes we are healed. (Isa 53:5)

Deeper Connections (Preferred Answers)

  • Q: What was the purpose of the Law of Moses?

    A: To teach Israel how to love and honor God and their fellow man, and to reveal God's plan of redemption to the world.

  • Q: Why did God demand that a sacrifice of an innocent be made for a guilty sinner?

    A: To teach that for sins to be forgiven, an innocent sacrifice had to die; this foreshadowed Jesus' perfect sacrifice.

  • Q: Why would God allow Israel to make bad choices (wanting a human king) instead of just telling them what to do all the time?

    A: Because God desires a relationship based on free-will choice and motivated by love, not forced duty.

  • Q: What evidences does the Bible use to show that Jesus would be part of God’s plan to redeem man from sin?

    A: Prophecies (e.g., the "Passover lamb" and Isaiah 53) and the sacrificial system that required innocent blood for the remission of sins.

Lesson 4: The Life of Jesus

True or False

  • 1. Since most people were factory workers, Jesus often taught lessons that used machines as examples. — False
  • 2. Jesus performed miracles to show that He had power and authority from heaven. — True
  • 3. The Jewish religious leaders usually liked Jesus. — False
  • 4. Jesus Christ sinned, just like every other person on Earth. — False
  • 5. The bread and the fruit of the vine (grape juice) magically turn into Jesus’ body and blood. — False
  • 6. The Jewish religious council gave Jesus a fair trial. — False
  • 7. Pilate was brave and refused to be bullied by the crowd. — False

Search the Scriptures

  • 1. “But you, O Bethlehem… from you shall come forth for me one who is to be ruler in Israel, whose coming forth is from of old, from ancient days.” (Mic 5:2)
  • 2. “And the Holy Spirit descended in bodily form like a dove upon Him, and a voice came from heaven which said, ‘You are My beloved Son’; in You I am well pleased.” (Luk 3:22)
  • 3. And as they were eating, Jesus took bread, blessed and broke it, and gave it to the disciples and said, “Take, eat; this is My body.” (Mat 26:26)
  • 4. O My Father, if it is possible, let this cup pass from Me; nevertheless, not as I will, but as You will. (Mat 26:39)
  • 5. Then Jesus came out, wearing the crown of thorns and the purple robe. And Pilate said to them, “Behold the Man!” (Joh 19:5)

Deeper Connections (Preferred Answers)

  • Q: Why does it matter that Jesus fulfilled prophecy?

    A: It validates that He is the long-awaited Messiah and Redeemer and that His life was part of God's divine plan.

  • Q: Why does it matter that Jesus did not sin?

    A: A sinless life was required because God's pattern for sacrifice demanded a pure and spotless offering to redeem the sins of others.

  • Q: What do the symbols of the communion represent?

    A: The bread represents Jesus’ body and the fruit of the vine represents His blood, shed for the remission of sins.

  • Q: Who is responsible for condemning Jesus to death?

    A: While the Jewish leaders and the Roman governor Pilate played direct roles, the Bible also notes that it was God's plan for Jesus to be the sacrifice for the redemption of all mankind.

Lesson 5: Death and Resurrection

True or False

  • 1. Everyone (including Jesus) has sinned. — False
  • 2. We are reconciled to God through Jesus’ death. — True
  • 3. The Resurrection proves that Jesus really came from Heaven. — True
  • 4. Saul (Paul) considered himself “the worst sinner” who ever lived. — True
  • 5. We can earn our salvation through works of merit alone. — False
  • 6. Faith is when something is planned by God and is worked out (accomplished) by man. — False
  • 7. Without faith, being baptized is just getting wet! — True
  • 8. Anyone in the world can become a Christian. — True

Search the Scriptures

  • 1. “My God, my God, why have You forsaken Me!” (Mat 27:46)
  • 2. “And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and hers; He will crush your head, and you will strike his heel.” (Gen 3:15)
  • 3. If we have hoped in Christ in this life only, we are of all men most to be pitied. (1 Cor 15:19)
  • 4. Here is a trustworthy saying that deserves full acceptance: Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners—of whom I am the worst. (1 Tim 1:15)
  • 5. For by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves... it is the gift of God, not of works, lest anyone should boast. (Eph 2:8-9)
  • 6. Corresponding to that, baptism now saves you—not the removal of dirt from the flesh, but an appeal to God for a good conscience... (1 Pet 3:21)

Deeper Connections (Preferred Answers)

  • Q: In your own words, why did/does the death of Jesus matter?

    A: Jesus’ death as the innocent and perfect sacrifice redeemed man from sin and allows us to be reconciled to God.

  • Q: In your own words, why does the resurrection of Jesus matter?

    A: It proves His deity, validates the Bible's message, demonstrates God's power over death, and offers man the hope of eternal life.

  • Q: Why do you think someone who tried to work for a salvation of merit would be offensive to God?

    A: It implies that salvation is something they deserve rather than accepting it as God's precious, unmerited gift.

  • Q: What is the difference between works of merit and obeying God?

    A: Works of merit are attempts to earn a reward; obeying God is an act of faith and trust in response to His grace.

  • Q: How does a person become a Christian?

    A: By hearing the gospel, believing in Jesus, repenting of sins, confessing Christ, and being baptized for the remission of sins.

Lesson 6: Until Jesus Comes Again

True or False

  • 1. Hell is only for the really bad people. — False
  • 2. God does not have a “rulebook” to determine which sins are worthy of Hell. — False
  • 3. All sins—if not forgiven—no matter how we may view them, are worthy of spiritual death in God’s eyes. — True
  • 4. Jesus took away the fate of Hell for anyone who believes and obeys. — True
  • 5. We can learn exactly what Heaven looks like. — False
  • 6. In Heaven, we will live as we were meant to be: in perfection and in God’s presence. — True
  • 7. I am ready for Jesus to come back. — True

Search the Scriptures

  • 1. “Enter by the narrow gate, for wide is the gate and broad is the way that leads to destruction, and there are many who go in by it. Because narrow is the gate and difficult is the way which leads to life, there are few who find it." (Mat 7:13-14)
  • 2. "O wretched man that I am! Who will deliver me from this body of death? I thank God—through Jesus Christ our Lord!" (Rom 7:24-25)
  • 3. "And God will wipe away every tear from their eyes; there shall be no more death, nor sorrow, nor crying. There shall be no more pain, for the former things have passed away." (Rev 21:4-5)
  • 4. "There shall be no night there: They need no lamp nor light of the sun, for the Lord God gives them light. And they shall reign forever and ever." (Rev 22:5)

Deeper Connections (Preferred Answers)

  • Q: How should we live since we do not know exactly when Jesus will come back?

    A: We should live for Him and maintain a lifestyle of faithfulness until His return.

  • Q: Why do sinners go to Hell?

    A: Hell is a place of punishment, destruction, and eternal banishment from God for those who do not have their sins removed through the blood of Jesus.

  • Q: Does anyone deserve to go to Heaven?

    A: No; being a good person does not remove sin. Only God's grace and an obedient faith in Jesus Christ can save us.

  • Q: What makes Heaven so special?

    A: It is a place of perfection where all symptoms of sin (pain, death, sorrow) are removed, and the relationship between God and man is completely restored.
